Who doesn't love a good visualization?
I've heard many times that "spatial data is special" and now I believe it.
Here you will find some of my projects in the form of webinars (usually in spanish), articles and web-apps.

I presented a geostatistical analysis of the historical precipitation in Nicaragua at the Women in Data Science (WiDS) conference. If you're interested in spatial histograms, voronoi clusters, space time cubes and emerging hotspot analysis: here's the link to the slides

Land cover and crop type classification

I used the geemap web-app to create this Time-Lapse of the water reservoir Las Canoas. The gif is of made of anual Landsat images for over 30 years. This allows to identify 2 key moments: between 1985 and 1986 the reservoir was built and in 2016 there's a severe drought.

Monitoring the Renaturation of The River Aa with UAV's

Another cool webinar in spanish, this time as part of a remote sensing course provided by the University of Costa Rica (UCR). I talk about global monitoring systems in agriculture, my personal experience with the calibration of the Agricultural Stress Index System (ASIS) of FAO, and I redirect you to some hands-on training channels available on youtube.
